A spacefaring billionaire — *that* one — face-down in a taverna's brine barrel. You cannot bury a name like this. Someone tried anyway: a "diving accident" release went out before we were even called. That's what caught our eye. Whoever kills a story that fast is standing over a body.

Six now, and we finally *see* it: every weapon a food, and every food the name of the **place** it happened. Not a lunatic with a pantry. A professional with a map — and, somewhere, a list we haven't got our hands on.

**THREAD · "ROME"** — *(current leading theory)*
Something Italian keeps surfacing at the edge of the file. The room likes it. Consensus is hardening: the next city is Rome. Assets requested for Italian culinary events across the week. We are, at time of writing, very sure.

**CASE 007 · SYDNEY, AU** — *the seventh · days ago · we were in the building*
Anonymous tip; a unit was inside the shopping centre minutes out. Too late for the auctioneer — shucked on the harbour rocks — but **not** too late to see the Grocer, who was *not alone.* A second figure, unidentified, gone before we reached the level. Both bolted. In the run, the Grocer ditched a shopping basket. Bagged on scene: **Exhibits A, B, C.** First hot trail this case has ever had.

**EXHIBITS A–C** — *recovered Sydney; see attached*
- **A** — a shopping basket. Contents: a packet of ravioli, a bunch of spring onions, a spray of cut flowers, and a handwritten **shopping list** folded in beside them.
- **B** — more pasta. Farfalle, this time. (One of the analysts keeps insisting it "can't have fallen far." He has been asked to leave the room.)
- **C** — an Italian cookbook.
Assessment: A, B and C all point one way. *Italy.* Rome is now the operating assumption; teams deploy this week.

**STATUS · OPEN**
